# Green et al. - Queueing theory for ED staffing

## Why we care
This paper is useful because it:
- explicitly studies time-varying arrivals
- uses queueing ideas to reallocate provider hours
- shows that staffing should follow demand shape, not just total volume

## Practical takeaway for our project
We should not use one constant arrival rate for every hour if we want a believable ED model.
Instead:
- use hourly or blockwise arrival rates
- at minimum, use piecewise constant rates over the shift

## Caveat
Their model is closer to provider-access delay than to a full triage subsystem simulation, so we should cite it as motivation rather than copy its exact structure.
